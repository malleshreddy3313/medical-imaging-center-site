About CMIS

Dr. Birendra Biswal has established a center in 2019 titled “The Centre for Medical Imaging Studies (CMIS)” research lab by collaborating with different hospitals such as “Gayatri Vidya Parishad Institute of Health Care and Medical Technology, India”, “Dristi Eye Care System”, Nepal, Kathmandu, Simadri Surya Eye Hospital, Visakhapatnam and “Asian Institute of Nephrology and Urology (Vizag)” Pvt. Ltd. (AINU), India to develop state-of-the-art algorithms in the area of ophthalmology and Prostate for the survival of humanity. In this center, Dr. Biswal works as the lead mentor and advisor to work on different radiological services and other bio-imaging services such as Fundus, ultrasonography, mammography, magnetic resonance imaging (MRI), and computed tomography images etc. As a result of this, the Science and Engineering Research Board (SERB) under the Department of Science and Technology (DST) has sanctioned a core research grant (CRG) to the tune of Rs. 21,73,800 having file no. EMR/2017/000885 to carry out the project work for three years to develop robust algorithms for the detection of NVD & NVE in diabetic retinopathy. As a by-product of this project, a good number of scholarly manuscripts have been published in different core International Medical Journals of repute with excellent coverage and a reasonably good impact factor. All the articles are published in the USA & UK, having excellent coverage under the Science Citation Index (SCI). Apart from this, CMIS has conducted several national workshops relevant to Image and Signal Processing funded by DST and CSIR, New Delhi, in the last five years. In CMIS, Dr. Birendra Biswal has been guiding and mentoring both B.Tech. and M.Tech. students and inculcating the interest of research in them. As a result, 10 B.Tech students have registered for doing M.S/M.Tech in reputed institutes like the University of California, IITs, NITs, etc. Further, 2 M.Tech students have registered for doing Ph.D. in the same area of Ophthalmology in reputed institutes like the University of Auckland, IIT Delhi, etc. Currently, a new project CRG project titled “A novel hardware embedded multivariate feature enabled health monitoring system for early diagnosis of Glaucoma,” is being carried out in the center in collaboration with hospitals like Simadri Surya Eye Hospital and institutes like IIIT Bhubaneswar, IIT Bhubaneswar, etc.

Vision

The vision of the “Center for Medical Imaging Studies (CMIS)” is to become one of the futuristic centers in developing a robust expert diagnostic system and providing timely, cost-efficient, and high-quality medical imaging & imaging-guided solutions for a diverse patient population.

Mission

  • To promote interdisciplinary and collaborative research in the healthcare domain using cutting-edge technologies.
  • To develop robust, cost-effective diagnostic systems using medical imaging technologies.
  • To sensitize, educate & empower low-income people through technology-enabled community development programs.
